03. Engineering Principles
01
Instrument before optimizing

Observation first, then targeted change. I make decisions based on metrics and tracer profiles, never by speculating where system bottlenecks live.

02
Design for the operator

A system is only as good as its operability. Observability, structured logging, safe rollback scripts, and simple restart metrics are standard deliverables.

03
Prefer boring solutions

I prioritize mature, robust, boring solutions over hype. I adopt new technologies only when I can describe the exact engineering limit existing stacks cannot resolve.

04
Think in constraints first

Every software design is a compromise between safety, storage, and speed. I start by outlining what can break, what must be consistent, and build from those truths.

05
Pain-driven development

If I do not know the underlying operational pain, I make mistakes. Real-world constraints and system failure points drive my design choices (PDD).

06
Assume external failure

The network is unstable, disks write slowly, and integrations drop offline. I design services assuming every external boundary will fail, configuring circuit breakers and fallbacks from day zero.
